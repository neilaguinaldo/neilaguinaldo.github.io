Climate is Everything


The global climate catastrophe is a real-time disruption of everyday life and livelihood, habitat destruction and resource depletion, loss of important ecosystems, and interrelated nonhuman and human ecologies.

The atmosphere, biosphere, hydrosphere, lithosphere, and biogeochemical cycles all interact with the behavior and activities of all lifeforms, including humans, to produce Earth's climatic behavior.That is, human actions and behaviors influence climate change; what we do has an impact on the habitability and livability of all species on Earth. Climate affects humans.

When land-use change (e.g., deforestation, mining, overfishing, loss of variety), industrial livestock and farming practices, which now consume half of the planet's habitable land, rupture biospheric, hydrospheric, and lithospheric processes, a climate in crisis occurs.

Behavior toward the world is driven by destructive activities based on supremacist myths. These narratives encourage aloof, apathetic, and ignorant perspectives that prolong existential dread, fear, despair, helplessness in the face of environmental harm, and intense sadness over planetary damage and loss of home and life.

That is, human actions and behaviors influence climate change; what we do has an impact on the habitability and livability of all species on Earth. Climate affects humans.

Climate phenomena are caused by the invisible interactions between each lifeform's behavior and activities, ecological processes, connected systems, and cycles.
